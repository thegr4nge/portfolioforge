  <behavior>
    CostBasisLedger maintains a dict[str, list[OpenLot]] (keyed by ticker). Each call to buy() appends an OpenLot. Each call to sell() removes from the front of the queue (FIFO) returning a list[DisposedLot].

    Test cases (RED before GREEN):

    1. **buy_adds_lot_to_queue** — after buy("VAS.AX", lot), len(ledger._lots["VAS.AX"]) == 1
    2. **sell_whole_lot_returns_disposed** — buy 100 shares then sell 100; returns 1 DisposedLot with acquired_date from the OpenLot
    3. **sell_partial_lot** — buy 100 shares at $90 total; sell 60. Returns 1 DisposedLot with quantity=60, cost_basis_aud=54.0 (60% of 90). Remaining lot has quantity=40, cost_basis_aud=36.0.
    4. **sell_multiple_lots_fifo** — ATO fixture C: buy 100 at $9000 (lot 1), buy 100 at $6000 (lot 2), sell 100. Returns 1 DisposedLot from lot 1 (acquired_date earliest). Lot 2 remains open.
    5. **sell_across_two_lots** — buy 50 at $50 cost, buy 50 at $60 cost, sell 80. Returns 2 DisposedLots: first (50 shares, $50), second (30 shares, $36 = 60% of 60). Remaining lot 2 has 20 shares at $24.
    6. **sell_exceeds_position_raises** — after buying 100, selling 101 raises ValueError mentioning the ticker
    7. **float_tolerance_no_false_error** — buy 100.0 shares; due to floating-point residual sell 99.9995 shares (remaining 0.0005 < 0.001 tolerance). Must NOT raise ValueError.
    8. **cost_basis_usd_none_for_aud_tickers** — OpenLot with cost_basis_usd=None propagates to DisposedLot.cost_basis_usd=None after sell.
    9. **multiple_tickers_independent** — buy VAS.AX and VGS.AX lots; sell VAS.AX only; VGS.AX lot untouched.

    Implementation notes:
    - Use `list[OpenLot]` not `deque` (partial lot mutation requires list)
    - Tolerance: `if remaining > 0.001: raise ValueError(f"Cannot sell {quantity} of {ticker}...")`
    - Partial lot proportional split for cost_basis_usd: `lot.cost_basis_usd * proportion if lot.cost_basis_usd is not None else None`
    - `_lots` defaults to `defaultdict(list)` from `collections`
  </behavior>
