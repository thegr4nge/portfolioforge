# PortfolioForge — Sales Brief
*Read this first. Everything else in this folder builds on it.*

---

## What it is

PortfolioForge is a software tool that produces **CGT (capital gains tax) workpapers for SMSF accountants**.

An SMSF is a self-managed superannuation fund — Australia has about 600,000 of them. Every one with share investments needs a CGT calculation done every financial year. Accountants do this manually in spreadsheets. It's slow, error-prone, and the output rarely satisfies auditors.

PortfolioForge takes a broker CSV (a trade history export from CommSec, SelfWealth, Stake, or IBKR), runs the ATO-correct calculation, and exports a **Word document** the accountant can attach directly to the client file. The whole thing takes under 10 seconds.

---

## Who buys it

**SMSF accountants** — specifically boutique/specialist practices with 10–100 SMSF clients. They handle the compliance side: tax returns, CGT calculations, liaising with auditors. They're CAs or CPAs, often sole principals or small teams. They're time-poor and technically sophisticated.

**Not** financial advisers. Not investors directly. The accountant is the buyer.

---

## The one-liner

> "SMSF accountants send us a broker CSV and get back an ATO-validated CGT workpaper in Word — every disposal traced to the specific ATO rule — in under 10 seconds."

---

## Why they buy

- **Time saved**: 2–4 hours per client per year doing it manually. At $150–$250/hr billing rate, that's real money.
- **Audit-readiness**: The Word export cites specific ITAA provisions for every calculation. Auditors can verify every number.
- **Accuracy**: Most manual calculations get ATO loss-ordering wrong. PortfolioForge hard-wires the correct rules, including the 33.33% SMSF discount (not 50% — that's the individual rate, and getting it wrong is one of the most common errors in the industry).

## What you need to know to sell it

1. SMSF = self-managed superannuation fund
2. CGT = capital gains tax, calculated every financial year on share disposals
3. FIFO = "first in, first out" — the ATO-mandated method for matching buy and sell parcels
4. The product outputs a **Word document** — accountants care about this, it fits straight into their workflow
5. The CGT calculations are validated against **ATO published examples** — this is the credibility anchor
6. The product is **not financial advice** — it's a compliance tool for professionals
